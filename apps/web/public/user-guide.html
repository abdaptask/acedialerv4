01 · Getting started

Install & sign in

IT will email you a download link. You can also use ACE Dialer in any browser at the address IT provides — no install needed.

  • Windows: run ACE Dialer Setup.exe and follow the prompts.
  • Mac: open the .dmg, drag ACE Dialer into Applications, and launch it.

Signing in

ACE Dialer uses your ApTask Microsoft account — the same login as Outlook.

  1. Open ACE Dialer and click Sign in with Microsoft.
  2. Choose your @aptask.com account and approve access on first sign-in.
  3. You stay signed in until you explicitly sign out.
Before you start

If you used Pulse or another softphone, let IT remove it first. Running two softphones on the same line causes double-ringing and dropped calls.

The dot at the top of the window shows your connection: a green Online means you're ready to make and take calls.

02 · Making calls

Placing a call

You can dial from anywhere in the app:

  • Keypad: type or paste a number and press the green call button (or Enter). Formatting like (973) 555-1234 is cleaned up automatically.
  • Recents: tap any past call to redial. Search by name or number at the top.
  • Favorites: tap a saved contact to dial.
  • A text thread: tap the phone icon at the top of any conversation.

International & IVR menus

Type + and the country code (or paste an international number — the country flag appears automatically). To navigate a "press 1 for sales" menu, add commas for pauses:

+18005551234,,1,3 — waits, presses 1, waits, presses 3.

03 · Receiving calls

When someone calls you

A full-screen call window pops up — even if ACE is minimized — your computer rings, and you get a desktop notification. You'll see the caller's name (from your Favorites or from JobDiva) or their number if unknown.

OptionWhat it does
AcceptPick up the call.
DeclineStop the ringing and send the caller to voicemail.
Hold & AcceptAppears when you're already on a call — puts the current call on hold and answers the new one.
ReplyDecline and send a quick text instead (e.g. "Can't talk, will call back"). Templates are editable in Settings.

04 · During a call

In-call controls

ControlWhat it does
MuteSilences your microphone; the caller can't hear you.
HoldPuts the call on hold (with hold music if you've set one). Tap again to resume.
KeypadOpens a number pad for entering menu options or extensions mid-call.
AudioSwitches your microphone and speaker (headset, USB phone, etc.).
RecordRecords the call; the recording is saved to your account.
TransferSends the call to another number.
Add CallDials a second person to start a 3-way conference.
MessageOpens the text thread with this caller.
Hang upEnds the call.

3-way conference

  1. On a call, tap Add Call and dial the second person — the first call goes on hold.
  2. When they answer, tap Merge to bring everyone together.
  3. You can mute individual participants, or hang up one leg without ending the whole call.

Transferring

Tap Transfer and enter the number. The call is handed off and your leg drops once the transfer connects.

If two calls overlap

Answer a second call while dialing out and ACE automatically puts the other call on hold, so only one call is ever connected to your mic and speaker.

05 · Texting

SMS & MMS

Send and receive texts and picture messages from your business number.

  1. Open Messages and tap New message (or an existing thread).
  2. Enter the number or pick a contact, type your message, and send with Enter.
  3. To attach a photo or file (MMS), tap the attachment icon in the compose row.
  • Quick replies: set up common phrases in Settings → Quick replies and insert them with one tap while composing.
  • Search across all threads by name, number, or message text.
  • Call from a thread: tap the phone icon at the top right.

06 · Voicemail

Listening & greetings

  • Listen: open Voicemail, tap a message to expand, and press play. Speed controls (1×, 1.5×, 2×) let you listen faster.
  • Transcripts: every voicemail is transcribed automatically — the text appears a few seconds after the message is left.
  • Search covers the caller's name, number, and the transcript — so you can find "the one about the proposal" even without remembering who called.
  • Call back with the phone icon on any row; mark read/unread with the dot icon, or select several and update them in bulk.

Your greeting

In Settings → Voicemail you can set what callers hear — either record/upload an audio greeting or type a message and have it read aloud. You can set a separate greeting for when you're busy versus when a call rings out.

Retention

Voicemails are kept for 30 days, then deleted automatically. Each row shows how many days are left. To keep one, download the audio from the player.

07 · Favorites & Recents

Contacts and call history

Favorites

Star a caller from Recents, or add one directly in the Favorites tab. Once saved, the friendly name shows everywhere — Recents, Voicemail, the incoming-call popup, during the call, and in message threads. Favorites sync across your devices.

Recents

  • Every incoming, outgoing, and missed call, newest first — with name, direction, duration, time, and status (answered, missed, voicemail, declined, blocked).
  • Missed calls show in red, with an unread count on the Recents tab.
  • Tap a name to see everything with that contact in one place — calls, voicemails, and texts.

08 · Internal chat

Message your colleagues

The Chat tab is 1-to-1 messaging with other ACE Dialer users at ApTask, with presence (online / away / offline) and unread badges.

Note

Internal chat stays inside the app — it does not send an SMS to a phone. Use the Messages tab to text external numbers.

09 · Microsoft Teams alerts

Notifications in Teams

Even when ACE Dialer is closed, you get a Microsoft Teams message when something reaches your line:

  • Missed calls — with the caller's number and a call-back button.
  • New texts — with a preview and a reply button.
  • New voicemails — with the transcript, plus listen and call-back buttons.

No setup needed. You can turn any of the three types off under Settings → Notifications.

10 · Multiple numbers

If you have more than one line

Some roles have more than one business number (for example a "Sales" and a "Support" line). If that's you:

  • The dropdown in the header picks which number you're calling from.
  • Calls, texts, and voicemails to any of your numbers come to you.
  • Each line has its own color and label so you can tell at a glance which one was used.

11 · Settings

Make it yours

Open Settings from the avatar menu (top right).

SettingWhat it does
ThemeLight, Dark, or match your computer.
Audio devicesChoose which microphone and speaker ACE uses.
NotificationsTurn desktop and Teams alerts on or off, per type.
Quick repliesManage the canned text replies used by "Reply" and messaging.
Call forwardingSend your calls to another number — always, or only when you don't answer.
Blocked numbersBlock a number so it can't ring you or text you.
Voicemail greetingRecord, upload, or type your greeting (see Voicemail).

Admins have additional tools (user management, reports, audit log) under Settings → Admin.

12 · Updates & tray

Staying current

  • The desktop app updates itself — when a new version is ready, a banner appears; click Restart to install (about 30 seconds).
  • Check manually any time from the avatar menu → Check for updates.
  • Closing the window (red ✕) minimizes ACE to the system tray / menu bar so calls keep coming. Right-click the tray icon → Open to bring it back, or Quit to fully exit (calls won't ring when quit).

13 · Troubleshooting & help

If something's off

No audio

Open Settings → Audio devices and pick the right mic and speaker, then test with a quick call.

Calls aren't ringing

Check the status dot at the top — it should be green Online. If it's red, sign out and back in, or quit from the tray and relaunch. Still stuck? Contact IT.

Sign-in fails

Usually a Microsoft configuration issue on IT's side — reach out to IT with the exact message you saw.

Need a hand?

Ask in the ACE Dialer Teams channel, or email support@aptask.com. For bug reports, include what you were doing, what happened, and your version number (shown under the ACE Dialer logo, top left).

ACE Dialer is ApTask's internal softphone, built on Telnyx and Microsoft Entra ID.
